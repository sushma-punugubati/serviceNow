## Story 1: Global Technology Company — From 47 Portals to One Service Experience

**Industry:** Technology  
**Challenge:** A Fortune 100 technology company with 120,000 employees had 47 different portals for requesting internal services — IT had its own portal, HR had three, Finance had two, Legal had its own, and each regional office had local variations. New employees averaged 3.5 days to get everything they needed on Day 1. The average employee used only 4 portals and didn't know the other 43 existed.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Enterprise Service Portfolio deployed across IT, HR, Finance, Legal, and Facilities
- Single Employee Service Center replacing all 47 portals
- Unified Service Catalog: 800+ catalog items from all departments accessible in one place
- Order Guide for new employee onboarding: one request triggers laptop, software licenses, access cards, parking, HR induction
- Service Portfolio: each department now maintains a portfolio of their services with named Service Owners

**Results:**
- New employee Day 1 readiness: 3.5 days → same-day provisioning
- Employee satisfaction with service delivery: 38% → 82%
- 47 portals retired — $3.2M in maintenance costs eliminated
- "Email IT/HR/Finance for requests" volume reduced 71%
- Service Owner accountability established for 340 services for the first time

**Key ESP Features Used:** Employee Service Center, Unified Service Catalog, Order Guides, Service Portfolio with Service Owners, ESM (multi-department)

## Story 2: Healthcare Network — Service Portfolio Revealing $18M in Redundant Services

**Industry:** Healthcare  
**Challenge:** A healthcare network formed from 8 merged hospitals had accumulated IT services from 8 separate organizations over 10 years. Nobody had a complete view of what services existed. When asked "what collaboration tools do we use?" the answer was: Webex, Teams, Zoom, Slack, Skype for Business, and 3 proprietary hospital systems — many serving the same user population.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Service Portfolio audit: all IT services documented for the first time
- Each service entered into ServiceNow Portfolio with lifecycle stage, service owner, annual cost, and user count
- Cost attribution enabled: total cost of each service calculated (licenses + infrastructure + support labor)
- Portfolio analysis: services categorized as Consolidate, Retain, or Retire

**Results:**
- 340 services documented — 47 classified as redundant (same capability, different tool)
- $18M in annual service costs eliminated through consolidation
- Collaboration tools: 7 → 1 (Microsoft Teams adopted enterprise-wide)
- Service Owner assigned for 100% of remaining 293 services
- Future mergers: new acquisitions will integrate into existing Service Portfolio within 60 days (standard onboarding process)

**Key ESP Features Used:** Service Portfolio Management, Service Lifecycle (Pipeline/Active/Retired), Cost Attribution, Service Owner Assignment

## Story 3: Retail Chain — Catalog Transformation Reducing IT Ticket Volume by 40%

**Industry:** Retail  
**Challenge:** A national retail chain with 50,000 employees had a service catalog that IT was proud of — 400 catalog items, detailed forms, comprehensive workflow. But only 30% of requests came through the catalog. The rest came via email, phone, walk-up desk, or Slack message. IT was spending 40% of Level 1 time manually creating tickets from these unstructured requests.

**Why the catalog wasn't used:** it was organized by IT team ("Infrastructure Team Requests," "Application Support Requests," "Security Team Requests"). Store managers didn't know which team managed which thing.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Full catalog redesign: reorganized from IT-team structure to user-intent structure
- New categories: "Get Equipment," "Set Up Access," "Fix Something," "Get Information"
- Catalog items renamed in plain language: "Get VPN Access" not "Remote Access Provisioning Request"
- 180 duplicate/redundant items retired — catalog reduced from 400 to 220 items (higher quality, more used)
- Mobile-friendly catalog deployed for store managers
- Order Guide for "New Store Opening" — single request provisions everything for a new location

**Results:**
- Catalog adoption: 30% → 74% of requests
- IT L1 time on manual ticket creation: 40% → 8%
- Average request fulfillment time: 6 days → 2.5 days
- "New Store Opening" Order Guide: cut provisioning time from 3 weeks to 4 days

**Key ESP Features Used:** Service Catalog Redesign, Order Guides, Mobile Catalog, Catalog Item Rationalization

## Story 4: Financial Services Firm — Service Portfolio Enabling Cloud Cost Governance

**Industry:** Financial Services  
**Challenge:** A large asset management firm had migrated 60% of its workloads to AWS and Azure over 3 years. But cloud services were provisioned by individual development teams without going through IT's Service Catalog. Monthly cloud bills arrived — nobody could explain what each line item supported. The CFO asked: "What business services do these cloud costs support?" — the answer was silence.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Cloud services added to Service Portfolio: each cloud-provisioned application registered as a Service
- CMDB Cloud Discovery enabled: AWS/Azure resources automatically discovered and linked to Service Portfolio records
- Service Catalog items created: "Request Cloud Environment (Dev/Test/Prod)" — teams must request through catalog, not ad-hoc
- Cost attribution: cloud spend tagged and attributed to Service Portfolio records automatically
- Service Owner responsible for approving cloud cost increases for their service

**Results:**
- Cloud cost visibility: for the first time, every dollar mapped to a service and a Service Owner
- Untagged cloud resources (previously 34% of spend): reduced to 3%
- Cloud waste eliminated: $4.1M in orphaned cloud resources identified and shut down
- CFO can now see: "Our trading platform costs $2.3M/year in cloud infrastructure"
- New cloud provisioning requests: 100% through catalog — shadow IT cloud spend eliminated

**Key ESP Features Used:** Service Portfolio, Cloud CMDB Integration, Service Catalog, Cost Attribution, Service Owner Governance

## Story 5: University — Student Service Portal Transforming Campus Experience

**Industry:** Higher Education  
**Challenge:** A large public university had separate portals for every department: IT, Registrar, Financial Aid, Housing, Library, Student Health, Campus Safety. Students had to navigate 12 different systems for routine requests. International students faced a 2-week delay getting everything set up before they could start classes. The university's student satisfaction scores ranked in the bottom quartile of peer institutions.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Enterprise Service Portal deployed: IT + Registrar + Financial Aid + Housing + Student Health in one place
- Student-oriented Service Catalog: organized by "I'm a new student," "I need to change something," "I have a problem"
- Order Guide: "New International Student Onboarding" — covers 18 actions across 6 departments in one request
- Service Portfolio view: each department's services tracked with SLA compliance and student satisfaction scores
- Knowledge integrated: before submitting a request, students see self-service articles that resolve 40% of requests without a ticket

**Results:**
- International student setup: 2 weeks → 2 days
- Student satisfaction with services: bottom quartile → top quintile in 18 months
- "Wrong department" tickets (students submitting to IT when the issue is Registrar): 35% → 4%
- Self-service deflection: 40% of potential tickets resolved via Knowledge articles surfaced in catalog
- Staff time on basic requests: reduced 55%

**Key ESP Features Used:** Enterprise Service Portal, ESM (multi-department), Order Guides, Knowledge Integration, Service Portfolio Dashboard, CSAT Tracking

## Story 6: Pharmaceutical Company — Service Retirement Program Freeing $12M in Resources

**Industry:** Pharmaceuticals  
**Challenge:** A global pharmaceutical company had accumulated decades of IT services. Many legacy services had been "replaced" by newer services but never formally retired — the old systems continued running, being maintained, and consuming licenses and infrastructure. IT leadership had no idea what percentage of their $380M IT budget was supporting services that were technically redundant.

**What ESP Solved:**
- Complete Service Portfolio audit: every service inventoried and linked to budget data
- Lifecycle classification: every service formally classified as Pipeline / Active / Retiring / Retired
- "Retiring" stage added to portfolio workflow: services earmarked for retirement must have: a retirement date, user migration plan, and data archival plan — tracked in ServiceNow
- Service Owner accountability: Service Owners responsible for executing retirements on schedule
- CMDB cleanup: retired services trigger automated decommission workflows in CMDB and ITAM

**Results:**
- 127 services identified as candidates for retirement (no active users or duplicate capability)
- $12M in annual IT costs freed in year 1 (licenses, infrastructure, support contracts)
- CMDB cleaned: 3,400 CI records decommissioned linked to retired services
- IT budget conversation changed: leadership now asks "what services are we retiring?" at every budget review
- IT staff redeployed from maintaining legacy systems to building new capabilities

**Key ESP Features Used:** Service Portfolio, Service Lifecycle Management, Retirement Workflows, CMDB/ITAM Integration, Cost Attribution
