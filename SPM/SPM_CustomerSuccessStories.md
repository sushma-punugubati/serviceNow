# ServiceNow SPM — Customer Success Stories

Real-world examples of organizations using ServiceNow Strategic Portfolio Management to align IT investments with business strategy.

## Story 1: Global Insurance Company — Eliminating the "Project Spreadsheet Jungle"

**Industry:** Financial Services / Insurance  
**Challenge:** A Fortune 500 insurance company managed 400+ simultaneous IT projects across 8 business units using Excel spreadsheets and email chains. The CIO had no single view of resource allocation — teams were overcommitted by 200% and no one knew which projects supported strategic goals.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Centralized all 400+ projects into SPM with a single portfolio hierarchy
- Implemented WSJF scoring to rank all demands automatically
- Resource Capacity Planning revealed that 60% of projects were assigned to the same 20 developers
- Scenario Planning showed the CIO: "If we fund 50 projects instead of 80, we can actually deliver them on time"

**Results:**
- On-time project delivery improved from 34% to 71% in 18 months
- Resource over-commitment eliminated — capacity planning made visible before commitments were made
- 42 low-value projects cancelled (freed up $6.2M in budget)
- Executive team adopted weekly portfolio review using SPM dashboards

**Key SPM Features Used:** Demand Management, Scenario Planning, Resource Capacity Planning, Portfolio Dashboards

## Story 2: Healthcare Network — Aligning IT Projects to Patient Care Goals

**Industry:** Healthcare  
**Challenge:** A regional hospital network with 12 hospitals had $180M in annual IT spend but couldn't answer: "Which of our IT projects actually improve patient outcomes?" IT projects were approved by IT leadership with no connection to clinical strategy.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Implemented Goal Frameworks in SPM linking every IT project to a clinical strategic objective (e.g., "Reduce readmission rates by 15%")
- RICE scoring added "Patient Impact" as a custom scoring dimension
- The Alignment Lens gave clinical leadership visibility into IT project portfolio for the first time
- Investment Objects tracked CapEx vs OpEx split — critical for healthcare finance

**Results:**
- For the first time, hospital CFO could see: "This $2M EHR project supports 3 of our 5 strategic goals"
- 23% of IT budget redirected from infrastructure maintenance to patient-facing digital tools
- IT satisfaction scores from clinical staff increased from 52% to 78%
- SPM's benefit tracking showed $14M in verified clinical efficiency gains linked to IT projects

**Key SPM Features Used:** Goal Frameworks, RICE Scoring, Alignment Lens, Investment Objects, Benefit Tracking

## Story 3: Global Retailer — Managing Agile and Waterfall in One Portfolio

**Industry:** Retail / eCommerce  
**Challenge:** A global retailer had 200+ developers split between traditional waterfall projects (store rollouts, ERP upgrades) and agile product teams (mobile app, website features). Portfolio reporting was impossible — agile teams reported in story points, waterfall teams in Gantt charts, and leadership couldn't combine them.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Implemented SPM Professional with native Agile Development integration
- Agile stories and sprints linked to Programs which linked to the Portfolio
- Hybrid Roadmaps showed waterfall milestones AND agile sprint deliverables on one timeline
- Resource assignments worked across both — a developer could be 50% on an agile squad and 50% on a waterfall infrastructure project

**Results:**
- Leadership got a single portfolio view for the first time — one screen, all 200+ projects
- Product throughput improved 31% (sprint-level bottlenecks visible earlier)
- "Watermelon projects" (green status, red reality) reduced — automated health calculations exposed 14 at-risk projects that were being reported as green
- Black Friday readiness reviews done in SPM instead of 3-day manual status collection

**Key SPM Features Used:** SPM Professional, Agile Development, Hybrid Roadmaps, Automated Project Health, Resource Assignments

## Story 4: Manufacturing Conglomerate — Strategic Portfolio Review for M&A Integration

**Industry:** Manufacturing  
**Challenge:** After acquiring 3 companies in 18 months, a manufacturing conglomerate had 6 separate project portfolios running with no integration. Each acquired company had its own PMO tools, its own project list, its own resource pool — and they were all duplicating work.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Migrated all 6 portfolios into one SPM instance
- Demand Management identified 47 duplicate or overlapping initiatives across companies
- Scenario Planning modeled "What if we consolidate ERP across all 6 entities?" — showed $18M savings vs. maintaining 6 separate ERPs
- CMDB integration (from ServiceNow ITAM) identified shared infrastructure assets to be rationalized

**Results:**
- 47 duplicate projects cancelled, saving $22M in combined budget
- Common platform selected and funded in 90 days instead of the 18 months projected
- Resource pool visibility across all acquired entities — first time leadership knew total headcount available
- Post-M&A integration completed 7 months ahead of schedule

**Key SPM Features Used:** Demand Management, Scenario Planning, Portfolio Consolidation, CMDB Integration

## Story 5: European Bank — Meeting Regulatory Demands with Portfolio Transparency

**Industry:** Financial Services / Banking  
**Challenge:** Under DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) and ECB regulatory requirements, a European bank needed to prove that every IT project supporting critical banking functions had proper risk assessment, funding justification, and executive sign-off — with a full audit trail. Their existing PPM tool couldn't produce the audit evidence regulators demanded.

**What SPM Solved:**
- SPM Demand Board approval workflow created an immutable record of every project decision (approved, rejected, deferred — all with reasons and approvers)
- Investment Objects provided full financial audit trail per project
- Integration with IRM linked IT projects to regulatory risk assessments
- Scenario Planning documented which projects were cut and why (critical for regulatory conversations)

**Results:**
- Regulatory audit passed with zero findings related to IT project governance
- ECB examiners praised the audit trail capability — "best we've seen from a bank of this size"
- Internal audit time reduced 60% — evidence already in SPM, not scattered across emails
- Board-level IT governance reporting automated — previously a 2-week manual process per quarter

**Key SPM Features Used:** Demand Board, Investment Objects, IRM Integration, Audit Trail, Scenario Planning

## Story 6: Telecommunications Company — Fixing the "Perpetually Green" Portfolio

**Industry:** Telecommunications  
**Challenge:** A telco's PMO was trapped in a culture where project managers self-reported status as "green" to avoid difficult conversations. Of 120 tracked projects, 118 were always green — yet 40% were delivered late and 35% over budget. Leadership had no confidence in portfolio data.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Automated Project Health calculation replaced manual status reporting
- Health calculated automatically from: milestone completion %, budget actuals vs. plan, time card hours vs. estimate
- AI Now Assist surfaced at-risk signals even before PMs were aware
- Mandatory time card entry for all project resources — actual hours became visible

**Results:**
- In the first month, "green" project count dropped from 118 to 67 — the others were now accurately showing yellow or red
- On-time delivery improved from 60% to 85% over 12 months
- Portfolio credibility restored — executives started using dashboards again
- $9M in budget overruns caught early enough to take corrective action (previously discovered post-completion)

**Key SPM Features Used:** Automated Project Health, Now Assist AI, Time Cards, Resource Utilization Dashboards

## Story 7: Government Agency — Demand Management Replacing Manual Budget Submissions

**Industry:** Government / Public Sector  
**Challenge:** A large federal agency received 800+ IT funding requests per fiscal year. The review process involved paper submissions, email chains, review committees, and Excel scoring sheets. The entire annual budget cycle took 6 months and still produced decisions that couldn't be justified in writing.

**What SPM Solved:**
- Ideas Portal replaced paper submissions — all 800 requests captured digitally with structured business case templates
- MoSCoW scoring (must/should/could/won't have) applied automatically based on regulatory mandate tagging
- Demand Board reviews conducted inside SPM — digital vote, approval reason captured
- Full audit trail from idea submission to funding decision — required for GAO compliance

**Results:**
- Annual budget cycle reduced from 6 months to 6 weeks
- 100% of funding decisions now have documented justification (zero GAO findings related to IT budget process)
- Lower-priority projects visible earlier — agencies could negotiate and reprioritize before commitments
- Stakeholder satisfaction with IT budget process improved from 31% to 74%

**Key SPM Features Used:** Ideas Portal, Demand Management, MoSCoW Scoring, Demand Board, Audit Trail
